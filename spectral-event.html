About

The Spectral Event is summit bringing together artists, technologists, and other fatidical minds August 5-6 in New Rochelle, NY. It will be a weekend of talks, workshops, performances and parties with practitioners and theorists working in creative technology, interactive media, VR/AR, design, urban planning, data science, and more. The goal is to bring a diverse cast of participants together to share ideas and spark discussions about the role that technology and new media can play in the process of building future communities.

Hosted by IDEA New Rochelle and The New Rochelle Downtown BID, the Spectral Event is a celebration of the burgeoning developments that are underway to transform downtown New Rochelle into an arts and technology district. Talks, panels, and performances by The Spectral Event Fellows including: Matt Romein, Sheetal Prajapati, Jason Sigal, Caroline Sinders, Kat Sullivan, Sam Tarakajian, Marco Antonio Castro, Nitzan Bartov, David Lobser, Todd Bryant, Stephanie Riggs, Allison Burtch, Allison Parrish, Sam Lavigne, Adrian Sas, Lisbeth Kaufman, Lajune MacMillan, Arwa Mahdawi, Pamela Liou, Cassie Tarakajian, Michele Cortese, Gabe Barcia-Colombo, Javier Molina and many more…

Saturday the 5th of August and Sunday the 6th of August there will be artist talks and panel discussions that are open to the public. They will be at the Ozzy Davis Theater at the New Rochelle Public Library beginning at 10:30 am each day. Please join us by taking the metro-north train to New Rochelle (the library is just a short walk from the station). The even classes and workshops have a limited number of spaces, and the signup is available at Gaddy Hall at Monroe College on a first come first serve basis to ticketed participants.

Why Spectral?

The first definition of the word "spectral" in Webster's Dictionary is: "of, relating to, or suggesting, a specter (ghost)." The second is "of, relating to, or made by a spectrum." Our "Spectral" refers to both of these senses.

We talk about specters as being spooky characters from the past, but we see no reason that ghostly visions shouldn't come to us from the future as well. When we try to bring about something that has never existed before, there is a period during which this new entity is still virtual, existing only as ghostly outlines in the imagination. We want our summit to bring forth new ideas into the world, and so we use the word spectral in this sense.

The Spectral Event is intended to bring us together to imagine how technology and art can shape new communities. Making a new community is a collective effort to focus the energies a diverse group of people as a prism does to the spectrum of visible light. We hope that you will also hold this in your mind when you hear "spectral."
